# How Consignment Works at Trainer Center

You have graded cards or collectibles you want to sell, but you do not want to deal with eBay listings, shipping, buyer disputes, or marketplace fees. That is exactly what consignment is for.

## What is Consignment?

Consignment means you bring your items to Trainer Center, we display them in the store, and when they sell, you get paid. You keep ownership of the items until they sell. We handle the display, the customer interaction, and the sale.

## What Qualifies for Consignment?

Not everything qualifies. Consignment works best for items that have clear value and will move in a retail setting:

- **Graded cards** (PSA, CGC, BGS slabs)
- **High-value raw cards** in excellent condition
- **Sealed product** (booster boxes, ETBs, special collections)
- **Premium collectibles** (rare figures, limited edition merchandise)

Bulk common cards or heavily played cards are not a good fit for consignment. For those, we can make you a direct buy offer instead.

## How the Process Works

1. **Call first.** Reach out to the store at (714) 951-9100 to discuss what you have. We will let you know if your items qualify and talk through pricing.

2. **Bring your items in.** We will inspect everything together, agree on display prices, and go over the consignment terms.

3. **We display and sell.** Your items go into the store where customers can see and buy them. We handle everything from that point.

4. **You get paid.** When your item sells, we pay you your agreed-upon share. Simple.

## Why Consignment Instead of Selling Online?

- No listing fees, no shipping hassle, no buyer disputes
- Your cards are displayed in a real store where serious collectors shop
- Staff can speak to the value and condition of your items in person
- You avoid the risk of shipping expensive cards through the mail

## Why Consignment Instead of Selling to Us Directly?

When you sell directly to a shop, you get paid immediately but at a lower price. The shop needs margin to resell. With consignment, you typically get a higher return because you are waiting for a retail buyer to pay closer to market value.

The tradeoff is time. Direct sale is instant. Consignment takes longer but usually puts more money in your pocket.
