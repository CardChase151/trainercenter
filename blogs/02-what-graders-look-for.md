# What Do Graders Look For? A PSA Grading Breakdown

You have a card you think is special. Maybe it is a vintage holographic Charizard or a modern alt art you pulled from a pack. You want to get it graded, but before you spend $25 to $300 on a submission, you should know what PSA is actually looking at.

PSA evaluates four things: centering, corners, edges, and surface. Your final grade is based on the weakest of those four areas. A perfect surface will not save you if your centering is off.

## Centering

This is the most common reason cards miss a PSA 10. Centering measures how evenly the border is distributed on all four sides of the card.

For a PSA 10, the front needs to be within 55/45 (meaning one side of the border can be at most 55% and the other 45%). The back is more forgiving at 75/25.

For a PSA 9, the front allows 60/40 and the back allows 90/10.

You can eyeball centering, but for accuracy, use a centering tool or app. There are free centering calculators online where you upload a photo and it measures the ratios for you.

## Corners

All four corners need to be sharp. Under magnification (10x is what PSA uses), there should be zero whitening, fuzziness, or bending. Even a tiny amount of wear on one corner can drop you from a 10 to a 9 or lower.

Hold the card at an angle under good light and inspect each corner closely. A jeweler's loupe is a cheap tool that makes this easy.

## Edges

Similar to corners but along the full length of each side. Look for whitening (white dots or lines along the edge), chipping, or rough cuts from the factory. Some cards come out of the pack with edge issues, which is why not every fresh pull is a guaranteed 10.

## Surface

This covers scratches, print lines, stains, dents, and any loss of original gloss. Holographic cards are especially prone to surface scratches that are invisible until you tilt the card under light.

Print lines are thin lines that run across the holographic surface. They happen during manufacturing and are not your fault, but PSA still counts them against the grade.

## PSA 10 vs PSA 9

A PSA 10 (Gem Mint) is essentially flawless across all four categories. A PSA 9 (Mint) allows one or two very minor imperfections. To most people they look identical, but the price difference can be massive. A PSA 10 Charizard might sell for 3 to 5 times what a PSA 9 goes for.

## How to Self-Evaluate Before Submitting

1. Check centering first. If it is visibly off-center, it is probably not hitting a 10.
2. Use a loupe or magnifying glass on all four corners and all four edges.
3. Tilt the card under direct light to check for surface scratches and print lines.
4. Use an AI grading tool like SnapGradeAI or PokeGrade to get a quick estimate before spending money on a real submission.
5. Be honest with yourself. If you see a flaw, PSA will see it too.
