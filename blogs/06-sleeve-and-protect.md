# How to Properly Sleeve and Protect Your Pokemon Cards

You pulled a great card. Now what? If you toss it in a binder pocket or leave it on a table, you are risking damage that drops its value immediately. Proper protection takes about 10 seconds and costs almost nothing.

## The Basics: Penny Sleeve + Top Loader

This is the standard for any card worth protecting. Two items, both cheap, and together they keep your card safe from bending, scratches, and moisture.

**Step 1: Penny Sleeve**

A penny sleeve is a thin, soft plastic sleeve. It fits snugly around the card and protects the surface from scratches. Slide the card in gently, top side first. Do not force it. These cost less than a penny each (hence the name).

**Step 2: Top Loader**

A top loader is a rigid plastic case. After the card is in its penny sleeve, slide the sleeved card into the top loader. The top loader prevents bending and provides a hard shell of protection.

That is it. Penny sleeve first, then top loader. This is how shops store their singles. This is how collectors ship cards. This is the minimum for any card you care about.

## Common Mistakes

**Putting a card directly into a top loader without a penny sleeve.** The card can shift around inside the top loader and the hard plastic edges can scratch the surface. Always use a penny sleeve first.

**Using binder pages for valuable cards.** Binder pages are fine for organizing a collection of commons and uncommons, but valuable cards can get scratched sliding in and out of the pockets. If it is worth more than a few dollars, it deserves a penny sleeve and top loader.

**Rubber banding stacks of cards.** This dents the edges of the outer cards. If you need to bundle cards together, use a card box or deck box instead.

**Leaving cards in direct sunlight.** UV light fades card surfaces over time, especially holographic cards. Store your cards away from windows and direct light.

## For Graded Cards

If your card is already in a PSA, CGC, or BGS slab, the case itself is the protection. You do not need to add anything. Just store the slab upright (like a book on a shelf) and avoid stacking heavy items on top of it.

## For Long-Term Storage

If you are storing cards you do not plan to access often, consider a card storage box. Put each valuable card in a penny sleeve and top loader, then stand them upright in the box. Keep the box in a cool, dry place. Avoid garages, attics, and anywhere with temperature swings or humidity.

## The Bottom Line

Penny sleeve, then top loader. That is the move. It costs under 10 cents per card and it protects your investment. There is no reason not to do it.
